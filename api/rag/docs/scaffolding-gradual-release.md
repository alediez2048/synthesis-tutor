# Gradual Release of Responsibility for Fractions

The I Do / We Do / You Do framework (Pearson & Gallagher, 1983) is one of the most effective scaffolding structures for fraction instruction. It aligns with Vygotsky's zone of proximal development: the tutor models, then shares control, then releases responsibility entirely.

## I Do (Modeling)

The tutor demonstrates the full procedure while thinking aloud. For splitting, narrate every decision: "I need to split this whole into 4 equal parts, so each part is one-fourth." Make the reasoning visible, not just the steps. Bruner's CRA framework recommends starting with concrete representations here — visual fraction bars or area models before symbols.

## We Do (Guided Practice)

Shift to collaborative problem-solving. Ask the student to predict the next step before executing it. For combining fractions, prompt: "We have 2/5 and 1/5 — what do you think we do with the numerators?" Correct gently and immediately. NCTM Principles to Actions (2014) emphasizes that this phase should surface student thinking, not just confirm right answers.

## You Do (Independent Practice)

The student works alone with the tutor monitoring. Intervene only when the student is stuck for more than one attempt or repeats an error pattern. Empson & Levi (2011) found that premature help during this phase undermines the development of fraction reasoning. Provide equivalence problems at varied difficulty to confirm transfer, not just repetition.

## Tutor Guidance

Transition between phases based on accuracy and confidence, not a fixed schedule. If a student regresses during You Do, loop back to We Do briefly rather than restarting from I Do.
