# Common Core 3.NF: Understanding Fractions as Numbers

The grade 3 Number and Operations — Fractions (3.NF) domain is where students first formally encounter fractions. The standards emphasize conceptual understanding over procedures.

## Key Standards

**3.NF.A.1** — Understand a fraction 1/b as the quantity formed by 1 part when a whole is partitioned into b equal parts; understand a/b as the quantity formed by a parts of size 1/b. This is the *unit fraction* foundation: every fraction is built from copies of a unit fraction (Lamon, 2012).

**3.NF.A.2** — Represent fractions on a number line. This is a deliberate shift away from area-only models. Siegler et al. (2011) show that number-line placement is a uniquely powerful predictor of fraction proficiency because it treats fractions as magnitudes, not just shaded regions.

**3.NF.A.3** — Explain equivalence and compare fractions using visual models. Students compare fractions with the same numerator or same denominator only — cross-multiplication and common-denominator algorithms are deferred to later grades.

## Splitting as a Core Action

Splitting — partitioning a whole into equal parts — is the generative action behind all of 3.NF. Confrey & Maloney (2010) identify splitting as a cognitive primitive distinct from additive reasoning, and argue it must be developed through hands-on partitioning tasks before notation is introduced.

## Tutor Implications

At the intro and tutorial phases, prioritize unit-fraction language: "This is one-fourth. Two-fourths means two copies of one-fourth." Avoid jumping to "two out of four" phrasing, which reinforces a part-whole-only interpretation and hinders later understanding of fractions greater than 1. If a student struggles with 3/4, ask: "How many one-fourths is that?"
