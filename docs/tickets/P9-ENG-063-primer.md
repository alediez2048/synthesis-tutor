# ENG-063 Primer: Motivation Fading

**For:** New Cursor Agent session
**Project:** Fraction Quest — Interactive AI-Powered Fractions Tutor for Ages 8–12
**Phase:** Phase 9: EdTech Research-Informed Redesign
**Date:** Mar 16, 2026
**Research:** Bardach & Murayama, 2025 — motivation transformation: extrinsic rewards as entry point, then fade as intrinsic motivation builds. Static reward systems interfere once competence develops.

---

## What Is This Ticket?

Implement motivation fading. Strong scaffolding and extrinsic support in early sessions; gradually replaced by self-explanation prompts and real-world challenges as competence develops. The tutor itself should fade — less hand-holding, more "What do you think?" and "Try it in the real world." Track session count or competence signals to adjust scaffolding and praise intensity.

---

## Key Changes (To Be Detailed)

- [ ] Define competence/session progression signals
- [ ] Reduce extrinsic cues (praise intensity, hint frequency) as competence grows
- [ ] Increase self-explanation and transfer prompts in later sessions
- [ ] Tutor verbosity and guidance level fade over time
